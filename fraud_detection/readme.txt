* Flagging potentially fraudulent studies 

Paul Glasziou's group working on better tools for systematic reviews.

Points to make:
- Our language model is specific to pre-clinical cancer papers and we aimed to target the templates they use. We'd likely need to create other language models for other fields, as the text-templates will be different. There are companies out there offering general screening for paper mills, but I think area-specific models will be much more sensitive. To train the model, you need to pick an area that's had a good number of retractions.
- Our model was built largely before the explosion of LLM-written papers which do not use a template and will be much harder to detect.
- There are other techniques to flag papers, such as unusual author networks which can be a strong indicator of a generated paper where authorships have been sold.
- For randomised trials there are tools like INSPECT-SR (from Jack Wilkinson and colleagues) and I know they are working on ways to automate that.
