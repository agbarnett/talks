# 2026 [Research Support Community Day](https://www.rscday.info/)

*Title*: AI slop is harming research

*Abstract*: AI slop is an oil spill in our digital oceans. Just like an oil spill, we don't know how deep the problem goes and cleaning it up is a difficult and time-consuming task. 

New AI tools allow unscrupulous researchers to create publication-quality papers in under an hour with little human input. Paper mills are using AI to mine open access datasets to produce thousands of papers that boost researchers’ CVs but add nothing to understanding. Some journals and preprint servers have become clogged with AI generated submissions; research funders will soon face the same problem.

To separate the sound from the slop, large changes are needed to our systems for reviewing and rewarding research. Researchers may need to leave time-stamped breadcrumbs of their progress to demonstrate that they did the work, for example ethics approvals and electronic lab notebooks. We need systems that are hard to fake, but easy for genuine researchers to comply with. These systems will be a large change to current practice and will require a shift in research culture.

A potential positive of AI is that a well-designed and validated model could “read” every paper ever published and give scores against key criteria, including competence and originality. This could create a welcome shift in the overall direction of research to move away from speed, volume and the impossible to define “excellence”, towards the more important goals of quality and competence.
