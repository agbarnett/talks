* Slow scholarship, what does it look like? For COPE (4 November 2026)

Precarity, competition and incentives all contribute towards a publishing environment in which research integrity can be compromised. As a counterbalance to this, how might pausing and considering scholarship benefit publication integrity in the long term? We’ll hear about initiatives that promote principles of slow scholarship and reflect on how this can lead to more robust findings and publication integrity.

15 min talk

Title: Slowing down to speed up scientific progress

Short summary: The "Publish or perish" mantra has been a disaster for science and thereby humanity. The pervasive pressure to get as many papers as possible in "top" journals has created a warped evidence base. Reproducible research is possible with pre-published protocols, code and data checking, and full and transparent reporting. However, this all takes additional time, and many researchers prefer to spend their time on their next paper rather than improving their current paper. New AI tools can speed up some research tasks and create additional time for researchers, but this newly available time might just be ploughed into more papers, further inflating the already crazy publication benchmarks. However, it might be possible to use the AI revolution to increase reproducibility if the AI tools that summarise research are trained to rely on research that is reproducible. Researchers who want their work to be influential would have an incentive to slow down and create robust and reliable evidence.
